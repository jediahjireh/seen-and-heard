HOW YOU CAN HELP:

The table above defines ways in which different sectors can contribute towards anti-trafficking.

SECTOR

INITIATIVES

ELABORATION

Business

Employment opportunities

Offer skill training to survivors of trafficking

Corporate Responsibility

Implement policies and practices that prioritise human rights and dignity

Train employees to recognise and report suspicious activities

Support anti-trafficking organisations

Host corporate social responsibility events

Invest in our mission (with finances, resources or time)

Healthcare

Training

Train service providers to recognise the signs of trafficking and respond appropriately

Provide tailored care

Advocate for healthcare policies that prioritise the needs of trafficking survivors

Accommodate for holistic support services to be trauma-informed

Information Technology

Raise awareness

Amplify the stories of survivors through digital platforms, harnessing tech for good.

Provide support

Offer technical support to anti-trafficking organisations.

Code for change

Develop technological solutions to leverage the power of technology, in order to rescue survivors and dismantle criminal networks

Cyber vigilance

Be on the lookout for online exploitation schemes

Education

Prevention

Emphasise the importance of healthy relationships with boundaries and clarify what they look like

Training

Educate school staff on the identification of and response techniques to potential trafficking situations

Syllabus Integration

Teach students about the dangers of exploitation and how to identify warning signs by integrating anti-trafficking educational modules

Legal

Representation

Provide legal assistance to trafficking survivors

Advocacy

Stand for stronger anti-trafficking laws and policies at all levels to enhance victim protections and hold traffickers accountable

Training

Undergo further training on human-trafficking identification signs and laws, as well as trauma-informed legal assistance

Investigation

Collaborate with stakeholders to dismantle trafficking networks and rescue survivors

Community engagement

Build trust with vulnerable populations to encourage the reporting of suspicious activity

Transportation

Training

Train workers to recognise and report signs of trafficking among potential cases they come across

Collaborate with law enforcement

Share information and coordinate efforts to identify and intercept trafficking activities in transit hubs and transportation routes.

Support provision

Provide transportation services to survivors to help them move to safe locations or access support services

Hospitality

Training

Train staff to recognise potential indicators of trafficking (such as guests with no luggage or unusual requests)

Awareness

Alert travellers about the risks of trafficking in common areas, as well as how to contact local authorities should they witness suspicious activity or require aid

Reporting systems

Establish protocols for reporting and responding to potential trafficking cases
